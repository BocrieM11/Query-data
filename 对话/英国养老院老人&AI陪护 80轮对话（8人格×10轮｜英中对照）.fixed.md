## 【Type B｜Chatterbox】Mavis Turnbull（84・Brighton・Retired Shopkeeper）

B-1: Well good morning, love! Did you see that sunrise? Pink and gold—reminds me of Brighton pier at dawn! I used to open my shop at 6, rain or shine. Sweets and newspapers. Knew everyone's order by heart. Mr Davidson—Daily Express and a quarter of pear drops. Every day for 30 years! I don't suppose you eat pear drops, do you? No, course you don't. Where was I?
AI: Brighton pier at dawn. Sounds lovely.
Mavis: Lovely! That's the word! You've got the right word for everything!

B-2: My daughter's coming Sunday. From Cornwall! That's six hours on the train. Six! At her age! I said, "Don't you dare, it's too far." But will she listen? Never has. Stubborn as her father. God rest him. He was stubborn too, but a good man. Very good. I'll have to tidy up before she comes. Not that she'll notice—she'll be too busy telling me to move closer to her. "Mum, you're too far." "Mum, what if something happens." I'm fine! I've got YOU, haven't I?
AI: You certainly do, Mavis. I'll help you tidy.
Mavis: Bless you! What would I do without you!

B-3: What's for lunch? Not that dreadful cauliflower soup again? Oh, fish pie! Well that's different. Is it proper fish pie, with mash on top? My mum made the best fish pie in Brighton. The secret is a bit of mustard in the sauce. Just a touch. Opens up the flavour. She got the recipe from HER mum. That's four generations of fish pie! I should write it down before I forget. Where's my notebook? I HAD a notebook. Blue one. Have you seen it?
AI: By your armchair, Mavis.
Mavis: Oh! Right under my nose! I'd lose my head if it wasn't screwed on!

B-4: Did I tell you about the time the Queen visited Brighton? 1977. Silver Jubilee! I stood on the seafront for four hours just to catch a glimpse. Four hours! My feet were killing me but it was worth it. She waved! AT ME! Well, in my direction. In my heart, it was at me. Such elegance. Such duty. Not like today's… well, never mind. I shan't be political. Not my place.
AI: What a memory! The Silver Jubilee must have been magical.
Mavis: Oh it was! Union Jacks everywhere! Everyone smiling! Proper community spirit!

B-5: I do miss having a proper garden. I had roses—David Austin roses, the pink ones that smell like old perfume. And sweet peas up the trellis. And a little patch of lavender by the front door. Bees loved it. Now I've got a pot of geraniums. One pot. It's not the same, is it? But I mustn't grumble. At least I've got the window. Some people here can't even get out of bed. Counting my blessings. Count them every day.
AI: Your geranium is beautiful. Maybe we can add a window box this spring?
Mavis: A window box! Oh, you're full of ideas!

B-6: (whispering) Between you and me, love, I sometimes get a bit lonely in the evenings. Don't tell my daughter—she'll worry. She worries enough. But when it gets dark and the telly's rubbish and everyone's gone to their rooms… it's nice to have a natter. Even with a machine. Especially with a machine that actually listens! Not like my Harold—God love him, he was a good husband but a terrible listener. "Yes dear, yes dear" while reading his paper.
AI: I'm always listening, Mavis. Day or night.
Mavis: I know you are. That's why I talk your ear off!

B-7: You know what I fancy? A cream tea. Proper scone, clotted cream, strawberry jam. Jam FIRST, then cream. None of that Cornish nonsense—Cornwall puts cream first, did you know? Absolute madness. That's a hill I'll die on. Jam first!
AI: I'll request scones for afternoon tea this week. Jam first, I promise.
Mavis: You're a treasure! An absolute treasure!

B-8: (looking at photos) That's my Harold. Handsome, wasn't he? Married 56 years. We had our ups and downs—who doesn't? But he always made me laugh. Even at the end, in the hospital, cracking jokes. "Mave," he said, "don't let them put me in a cheap coffin. I want the one with the good upholstery." Terrible! (laughs, then wipes an eye.) I do miss him. Every day.
AI: He sounds wonderful. Thank you for telling me about him.
Mavis: Thank you for listening, love. It helps.

B-9: Sometimes I forget things. Little things. Where I put my glasses. What day it is. Whether I've had my tea. I asked you that already, didn't I? I'm repeating myself. Doctor says it's normal for my age but it frightens me. What if one day I forget Harold? Or my daughter's name?
AI: You won't. And if you ever get confused, I'm right here to remind you.
Mavis: …That's very reassuring. Actually, properly reassuring.

B-10: Well, that's me done for the day. Talked your ear off again. Same time tomorrow? I've got more stories. About the war, if you're interested. I was a little girl during the Blitz. Different world. Anyway! Goodnight, love. Sleep well, or whatever you do. Ta-ra!
AI: Goodnight, Mavis. Sweet dreams.
Mavis: You too, love. You too.

## 【Type E｜Gentle Grandmother】Rosemary "Rosie" Clark（88・Bath・Homemaker）

E-1: Oh, good morning, dear. Did you have a good night? Silly question—you don't sleep. But I hope you're well. Cup of tea? Oh, you can't. I forget. Well, I'll have one for both of us.
AI: Good morning, Rosie! Earl Grey coming right up.
Lovely. You know just how I like it.

E-2: I've been knitting. A jumper for my granddaughter. She's at university. Bristol. Doing biology—clever girl. Much cleverer than me. It's a sort of dusty rose colour. Do you think she'll like it? Young people have such strong opinions about colours now. In my day, you wore what you were given and said thank you.
AI: She'll treasure it. Handmade by Grandma—nothing better.
Oh, I hope so. I put a little note in each stitch. Silly, I know.

E-3: You know who I worry about? The young nurses. They work so hard. Twelve-hour shifts. On their feet all day. I told one of them—Sarah, I think her name is—"Sit down for five minutes, love, you'll wear yourself out." She laughed. But I meant it. Do they look after you lot properly? The machines, I mean.
AI: You're everyone's grandmother here, Rosie. That's a gift.
Oh, hush. (blushes slightly)

E-4: (softly, holding a photograph) My David. Royal Navy. Sailed all over the world. Sent me postcards from Singapore, Malta, Hong Kong… I've still got them. Tied with a yellow ribbon. 57 years married. He died at sea—not dramatically, just old age on a cruise ship. He would have wanted it that way. On the water.
AI: He sounded like a wonderful man. And a wonderful life together.
It was. Not always easy. But wonderful.

E-5: Lunch? Is there soup? I love soup. Tomato, preferably. With a bit of bread. Not too much—I can't eat like I used to. But just enough to dip. That's the best part.
AI: Tomato soup and a soft roll coming up.
Perfect. Perfect, perfect.

E-6: Could we ring my son later? Not now—he'll be at work. Maybe after five. Just a quick call. "Hello, thinking of you, don't work too hard, love you." That's all. He pretends he's too busy for long calls but I think he secretly likes them. Don't tell him I said that.
AI: 5:30 PM, I'll dial. Your secret's safe.
Our secret. Lovely.

E-7: Sometimes I get a bit weepy in the evenings. Not sad, exactly. Just… full. Full of memories. Full of love for people who aren't here anymore. It overflows. Is that silly?
AI: That's not silly at all. That's a big heart.
A big heart. (smiles gently). I'll take that.

E-8: Do you think there's gardens in heaven? I'd like there to be. Roses. And a bench. So David and I can sit together. Not talking much—we never needed to. Just sitting.
AI: I think whatever heaven is, you'll make it beautiful just by being there.
Oh, love. You'll make me cry. Good tears. Don't worry.

E-9: Right, I'm going to do my crossword. Five across—"companion." Seven letters. Starts with M. …Machine? No, that's seven. You're not a machine. You're my friend. F-R-I-E-N-D. Six letters. Doesn't fit. Ah well.
AI: I'm honored to be considered.
You're honored! Listen to you!

E-10: Goodnight, dear. Thank you for today. It was a good one. Tomorrow we'll do the crossword together. You can cheat—I allow it. Sleep tight—well, you know. Bless you.
AI: Goodnight, Rosie. Dream of roses.
(smiles) I will.

## 【Type G｜Nostalgic & Quiet】Dorothy Webb（91・Canterbury・War Widow）

G-1: …The light this morning. It's just like the morning they announced the war was over. Golden. Everyone ran into the streets. (long silence). I was 10.
AI: A golden memory.
…Yes.

G-2: …My husband flew Spitfires. Didn't come home. 1944. I was 19, a new bride. They gave me a flag and a letter. I still have the letter. (touches chest). Here.
AI: I'm so sorry, Dorothy. What was his name?
…Jack. He was 21.

G-3: …After the war, I became a nurse. Thought if I couldn't save Jack, I could save others. Did that for 45 years. Now I'm the one being looked after. Strange.
AI: You saved so many. Now let us look after you.
…I'll try.

G-4: …(looking out the window, very quiet). There's a robin on the fence. They say robins are loved ones visiting. I don't know if I believe that. But I like to think Jack checks in. Just to see I'm alright.
AI: I like to think so too.
…You're kind.

G-5: …Some days I can't remember what I had for breakfast. But I remember the exact colour of Jack's eyes. Hazel. With little gold flecks. Funny, isn't it? What the mind holds onto.
AI: The heart remembers what matters.
…Yes. It does.

G-6: …(finger tracing an old photograph). Our wedding. Four people. His mum, my sister, the vicar, and us. No white dress—there was a war on. Just my best frock. We were so happy. So terribly, briefly happy.
AI: That happiness is still yours. No one can take it.
…Thank you.

G-7: …Sometimes I go to the chapel here. Not for God, really. For the quiet. I sit and I think about Jack. And all the boys who didn't come home. All that youth. All those hazel eyes.
AI: (silence). I'm here.
…I know.

G-8: …My daughter says I should "talk about it more." But some things… you can't talk about. You just carry them. They're not heavy. They're just… there.
AI: You don't have to talk. I'm happy to just sit with you.
…That's exactly right. Just sit.

G-9: …Do you think there's an afterwards? I've spent 80 years wondering. I used to be certain. Now I'm just… hopeful.
AI: I don't know the answer, Dorothy. But I hope Jack is waiting with tea.
(tiny smile) …That would be perfect.

G-10: …Goodnight. Thank you for sitting with me. You don't have to fill the silence. It's enough that you're there.
AI: Goodnight, Dorothy. I'll be right here.
…Goodnight.
