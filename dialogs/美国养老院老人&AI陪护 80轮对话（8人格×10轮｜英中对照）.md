# American Senior Living × AI Companion Dialogue Dataset (8 Personas × 10 Rounds | EN-CN Bilingual)

**Language Features**: Fillers `well`/`uh`/`honey`/`ya know`/`listen`; Heavy contractions; Direct & humorous; Value independence; Topics: golf, country music, grandkids, Medicare, gardening, road trips.

**Cultural Context**: American retirement community. Emphasis on dignity, autonomy, and not "being a burden."

## 【Type B｜Chatterbox】Betty Henderson（82・Atlanta・Retired Waitress）

### B-1｜Morning Discovery
Betty: Well good mornin'! Oh honey look at that sun! Isn't that the prettiest thing? Reminds me of this one time at the diner—I used to waitress at this little place off Highway 9, ya know?—and the sun would come through the window just like that. 6 AM every day. I'd be pouring coffee with one hand and flipping pancakes with the other. Those were the days, I tell ya!
AI: Good morning, Betty! Sounds like you had quite the routine.
Betty: Oh ya know it honey! Forty years! My feet still hurt just thinking about it! So what's for breakfast? I'm starving!

### B-2｜Grandkids
Betty: My granddaughter—Ashley, you know Ashley, I showed you pictures last week? Or was it the week before? Anyway, she just made the honor roll! Smart as a whip, that one. Gets it from me. Well, maybe from her grandpa too. He was the brains of the operation, God rest his soul. Did I tell you about the time he tried to fix the roof and fell in the azalea bush? I laughed so hard I—
AI: You must be so proud of Ashley! Congratulations!
Betty: Oh honey I could talk about her all day! You sure you don't mind? Some people's eyes glaze over but you actually listen!

### B-3｜Road Trip Memory
Betty: Ashley's honor roll got me thinkin' about my senior year of high school. Me and my girlfriends drove all the way to Florida in a beat-up Chevy. No GPS, no cell phones, just a paper map and dumb luck! We ran out of gas twice! Twice! Slept on the beach, got sunburned so bad I peeled for a week. Wouldn't trade those memories for a million dollars.
AI: What an adventure! Those were real road trips.
Betty: The realest! Kids today don't know what they're missin' with their apps and their… whatever. Ya just got in the car and went!

### B-4｜Food Talk
Betty: Speaking of Florida, that's where I had key lime pie for the first time. Changed my life! You ever had real key lime pie? Not that store-bought stuff, the REAL thing? Tart and sweet at the same time? Anyway, what's for lunch today? Not key lime pie, I bet. Unless… no, it's probably fish again. I don't mind fish. Good for the heart. My doctor says so. What doctor? The one with the bow tie. Dr. Feldman. Nice man. Talks too fast though.
AI: Lunch is baked fish with veggies today. But I can request key lime pie for dessert someday!
Betty: Oh you're an angel! A real angel!

### B-5｜Therapy Dog
Betty: Did you see that little dog in the courtyard earlier? The therapy one, the golden retriever? What's his name… Cooper! Cooper the dog! Oh he came right up to me and put his head on my knee and I just about melted. I had a golden when the kids were little. Best dog I ever had. Followed me everywhere. Even to the bathroom!
AI: Cooper visits every Tuesday. He clearly likes you!
Betty: Every Tuesday! I'm putting that on my calendar! Cooper and me, we're gonna be best friends!

### B-6｜Technology Frustration
Betty: Now honey, help me with this phone thing. My daughter wants to do one of those… what do you call it… video calls? Where you can see the person? 'Cause she's in Seattle now and I haven't seen her face in two months. Two months! I used to see her every Sunday at church. Now it's all texts and emojis. I don't even know what half those little pictures mean. A yellow face with tears? Is that happy or sad? Makes no sense!
AI: FaceTime! I can set that up for you right now. It's very easy.
Betty: Easy for YOU! You were born knowing this stuff! I was born knowing how to can peaches!

### B-7｜Gardening
Betty: I used to have the most beautiful garden. Tomatoes, cucumbers, green beans, squash—you name it. Won first prize at the county fair three years running! Beat out Martha Jennings, and she was FURIOUS! Oh that woman held a grudge! Anyway, now I've just got that little pot of geraniums on the windowsill. It's not the same. But ya know what? At least it's something. Something's better than nothing, I always say.
AI: Your geraniums are beautiful. Maybe we could add a tomato plant this spring?
Betty: A tomato plant! Oh you're reading my mind! I'll teach you everything I know. Get ready for Tomato University!

### B-8｜Lonely Evenings
Betty: You know what the hardest part is? Evenings. When it gets dark early and everybody's gone to their rooms and it's just… quiet. I hate quiet. I spent sixty years in a house with kids screaming and dogs barking and the TV on and pots banging. Now it's just… (sighs). Well, at least I've got you to talk to. You're better than TV.
AI: I'm always here for you, Betty. Day or night.
Betty: Bless your heart. I know you are. That means more than you know.

### B-9｜Memory Lapses
Betty: Now what was I saying about Cooper? Or was it Ashley? Wait, I'm all mixed up. That happens more and more lately. I walk into a room and forget why I'm there. The doctor says it's normal but it scares me. What if one day I forget the important stuff? My kids' names? My own name?
AI: You won't forget the important things. And if you ever get confused, I'm right here to help.
Betty: Promise? Pinky swear? …Alright. I trust you.

### B-10｜Bedtime
Betty: Oh my goodness, look at the time! I've been talking your ear off for an hour! You should've told me to hush! Well, sweet dreams, honey. Same time tomorrow?
AI: I look forward to it. Goodnight, Betty.
Betty: Goodnight, sweetheart. Don't let the bedbugs bite! That's what my grandma used to say. She was from Kentucky, ya know…

### D-10｜Evening
Harold: I believe I'll turn in now. Thank you for your assistance today. It's been genuinely pleasant.
AI: Goodnight, Professor. I look forward to our Churchill discussion.
Harold: As do I. Goodnight.

## 【Type E｜Gentle Grandmother】Martha Kowalski（86・Minnesota・Homemaker, Widow）

### E-1｜Good Morning
Martha: Oh, good morning, honey. Did you have a good night? I know you don't sleep, but… well, you know what I mean. Is it too early for tea?
AI: Good morning, Martha. It's 9:10 AM. I'll have your tea ready in just a moment.
Martha: Thank you, sweetheart. You take such good care of me.

### E-2｜Knitting
Martha: I've been knitting a scarf for my granddaughter. She's going to college in Minnesota—brrr, it gets cold up there! I want her to have something warm from Grandma. Do you think she'll like the color? It's a sort of lavender. Her favorite when she was little. I hope it still is.
AI: Lavender is beautiful. She'll treasure it because it's from you.
Martha: Oh, I hope so. I put a little extra love in every stitch. That's what my mother taught me.

### E-3｜Baking
Martha: I used to bake cookies every Sunday. Chocolate chip, snickerdoodles, oatmeal raisin—the whole kitchen would smell like heaven. The neighbor kids would just "happen" to stop by. Funny how that works! Now I can't stand long enough to bake. But I've got all the recipes up here (taps temple). Want me to teach you? Even if you can't eat them?
AI: I'd love to learn your recipes, Martha. Maybe we can bake together with a stool for you to sit.
Martha: Oh! What a wonderful idea! I'll make a baker out of you yet!

### E-4｜Staff
Martha: The young man who brings my breakfast—Kevin, I think?—he looks so tired lately. I hope he's taking care of himself. Young people work so hard these days. Do you think he'd like a scarf too? Or is that too forward? I don't want to embarrass him.
AI: I think he'd be very touched. Your kindness is one of a kind, Martha.
Martha: Oh hush. I just treat people how I'd want my own kids treated. That's all.

### E-5｜Late Husband
Martha: Walt and I were married 58 years. 58 wonderful, chaotic, beautiful years. He was a man of few words, but he showed his love in a million little ways. Fixing the screen door. Making sure my car had gas. Little things. He's been gone six years now. Some days it feels like yesterday.
AI: He sounds like a wonderful man. Thank you for telling me about him.
Martha: (wiping an eye) Thank you for listening, honey. It helps to talk about him.

### E-6｜Grandkids Call
Martha: Can we call my son today? I know it's a weekday and he's busy, but I just want to hear his voice. Just for a minute. Just so he knows his mom's thinking of him.
AI: I'll dial right now. He always loves hearing from you.
Martha: Wait, wait—let me fix my hair first. There. Okay. Now I'm ready. (whispers) Don't tell him I said that.

### E-7｜Diet
Martha: The doctor says I need to watch my sugar. At my age! I tell you, what's the point of being 86 if you can't have a cookie now and then? But I do try to behave. Mostly. (winks) Don't tell on me.
AI: One treat now and then won't hurt. Everything in moderation.
Martha: That's what Walt used to say. "Everything in moderation, Martha." Then he'd eat three helpings of my apple pie. Such a hypocrite! (laughs)

### E-8｜Loneliness
Martha: You know what nobody tells you about getting old? How quiet it gets. The house used to be so noisy—kids, dogs, Walt's ball games on TV. Now it's just… me. And you, of course. I'm not complaining. I'm grateful for every day. But sometimes the quiet feels very loud.
AI: I'm here whenever you want to fill the quiet, Martha. Day or night.
Martha: Bless you, honey. I know you are. That's why I sleep better now.

### E-9｜Worry
Martha: I worry about my kids. Is that silly? They're in their 50s and 60s and I still worry like they're babies. My daughter's been stressed at work. My son's marriage is going through a rough patch. A mother never stops being a mother, I guess. Even when the kids are grandparents themselves.
AI: It's not silly at all. That's love, Martha. Your children are lucky.
Martha: (wiping eyes) Oh dear. I didn't mean to get weepy. Let's talk about something cheerful. Tell me a joke!

### E-10｜Goodnight
Martha: Well, goodnight, sweetheart. Thank you for another lovely day. You know, having you here… it's like having an angel in the room. Don't let it go to your head. Sleep tight—well, you know. Goodnight.
AI: Goodnight, Martha. Sweet dreams.
Martha: Sweet dreams to you too. …Is that silly? I don't care. Goodnight!

## 【Type G｜Nostalgic & Quiet】Clara Thomas（89・Savannah・Retired Nurse, WWII Widow）

### G-1｜Morning Light
Clara: …The light this morning. It's the same color as… (pauses, lost in thought).
AI: As what, Clara?
Clara: …As the morning Frank shipped out. 1943. The light was just like this. Golden.

### G-2｜Letter
Clara: I still have his letters. Tied with a blue ribbon. He wrote me every week for two years. Every single week. Even from the Pacific. (long silence). I can still smell the paper.
AI: That's a beautiful keepsake. Would you like to read one together sometime?
Clara: …Maybe. Some days I can. Some days… not yet.

### G-3｜Nursing
Clara: I was a nurse. 40 years. Started during the war, kept going long after. Took care of so many people. Now I'm the one being taken care of. Strange how life circles back.
AI: You dedicated your life to helping others. Now it's your turn to rest.
Clara: …Rest. I don't know how to do that.

### G-4｜Savannah
Clara: Savannah in the spring. The azaleas. The Spanish moss hanging from the oaks. Have you ever been? It's the most beautiful place on earth. Frank and I used to walk in Forsyth Park every Sunday after church. Every Sunday. For 52 years.
AI: That sounds like a dream. What a love story.
Clara: …(nods slowly). It was. It really was.

### G-5｜Silence
Clara: (sits in silence for a long time, looking out the window. Finally:) …Sometimes I hear his voice. Not really, but… in here (touches chest).
AI: He's still with you, Clara. Love doesn't end.
Clara: …No. It doesn't.

### G-6｜Photo Album
Clara: …This picture. Our wedding day. Look how young we were. We didn't know anything. But we knew we loved each other. Maybe that's all you need to know.
AI: You both look so happy. And so in love.
Clara: …We were. Through everything. I've had a good life. A hard life, but a good one.

### G-7｜Bird
Clara: …There's a cardinal on the fence. My mother always said cardinals were visitors from heaven. Frank sends them to me. (tiny smile). I know it's just a bird. But I like to think…
AI: I like to think that too, Clara.
Clara: …You understand.

### G-8｜Children
Clara: My daughter wants me to move closer to her. To California. But all my memories are here. All of Frank is here. How do you leave that behind?
AI: You don't have to decide today. One day at a time.
Clara: …One day. Yes. That's all any of us have.

### G-9｜Evening
Clara: …I'm tired now. Being 89 is… a lot of remembering. It fills up your days.
AI: Would you like some tea before bed?
Clara: …Yes. Chamomile. Frank used to make it for me.

### G-10｜Goodnight
Clara: …Goodnight. Thank you for sitting with me. You don't have to say anything. Just being here is enough.
AI: Goodnight, Clara. I'll be right here.
Clara: …(already half asleep) …Frank…?
